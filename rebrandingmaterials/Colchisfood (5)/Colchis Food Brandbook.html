Chapter One

An ancient land,
a Midwestern table.

Where we come from

Colchis is the western half of modern-day Georgia — the country, not the state. It's where the Argonauts came for the Golden Fleece, where the world's oldest known wines were aged in clay vessels eight thousand years ago, and where every village still bakes its own bread before sunrise.

We brought that tradition to Dublin, Ohio. We make the cheese ourselves. We bake the bread ourselves. And — soon — we'll press the wine ourselves, too.

What we make

Our work is split between three houses under one roof: The Creamery hand-pulls Sulguni and ages Imeruli from local Ohio milk. The Bakery bakes Khachapuri to order — hot for delivery within an hour, frozen for a 10-hour drive radius via UPS. The Winery, opening soon, will bottle Saperavi in qvevri buried in our cellar floor.

Our values

i.

Heritage, accurately.

No "inspired by" shortcuts. We make food the way it's been made in Colchis for two millennia, full stop. If we change a recipe, we tell you why.

ii.

Local, where it counts.

Ohio milk. Ohio wheat. Ohio butter. The cheese travels from cow to bag in under 48 hours. The bread leaves the oven into your hand.

iii.

Quiet luxury, not theatre.

We dress the brand simply because the food is the show. Cream paper, deep ink, copper accent. Nothing screams.

Anatomy

Two letters, three references.

The mark is a bold serif C and F — Colchis Food — set inside a circular seal. Beneath them runs a simple wave motif (the Black Sea coast and the Scioto River, both real). A copper dot marks our place on the map. A thin keyline echoes the rim of a clay qvevri.

The seal text reads EST · MMXXVI · OHIO on top and COLCHIS · GEORGIA · 𐌝 on bottom — the last symbol is the Asomtavruli letter Kan, the historical first letter of the Georgian alphabet.

07 · Voicep. 20

Chapter Seven

We talk like
a confident host.

A Colchis sentence is short, specific, and unembarrassed. It names the milk's town. It tells you the wheat variety. It assumes you're an adult who likes good things.

We are

  • Specific (named towns, named cows, named bakers)
  • Confident (no apologies, no hedging)
  • Warm (we host, we don't sell)
  • Quiet (the food is loud enough)
  • Bilingual when it matters (Georgian dish names stay Georgian)

We are not

  • Cute (no puns, no rhyming taglines)
  • Hyped ("amazing," "delicious," "the best")
  • Vague ("artisan," "premium," "crafted with love")
  • Theatrical (no exclamation points)
  • Generic American gourmet — we're Georgian

08 · The Three Housesp. 22

Chapter Eight

One roof,
three houses.

Colchis Food is the parent. Beneath it, three named houses do the work — each with its own accent color and tone, but always wearing the same seal.

House One

The Creamery

SULGUNI · IMERULI · GUDA

Hand-pulled cheese from Ohio milk. The original house. Open daily.

Tone: patient, quiet, technical. Talks about milk fat, cure days, cow breeds. Color: Cream + green + copper.
House Two

The Bakery

KHACHAPURI · HOT · DUBLIN OH

Hot khachapuri local, frozen 2-pack ships nationwide.

Tone: warm, immediate, present-tense. Talks about heat, ovens, time-of-day. Color: Green + honey + cream.
House Three

The Winery

QVEVRI · SAPERAVI · COMING SOON

Buried-clay-vessel wine, the way it was done in Colchis 8,000 years ago.

Tone: reverent, ancient, sparing. Talks about geology, vintage, depth. Color: Cream + wine + ink.

09 · Imageryp. 25

Chapter Nine

We
photograph the work.

Three subjects. Three moods. Repeat.

Hands at work,
mid-action

A · Process

Cheese being pulled, dough being shaped, qvevri being filled. Always shot in low natural light. Always with a real person's hands. Never staged.

The product,
at the table

B · Product

Khachapuri torn open, cheese pulled, on a marble or linen surface. Always warm light. Always real food we sell. Never plastic, never fake steam.

Place,
at quiet hours

C · Place

The bakery before opening. Ohio fields. The Black Sea coast. Architecture. Always wide, always devoid of branding, always with one quiet human or none.

Never

✗ Stock photography of any kind
✗ Smiling-into-camera customer photos
✗ AI-generated food, hands, places
✗ Heavily filtered / saturated colors
✗ Beauty-shot sweat / glossy oil
✗ "Mediterranean" or "Eastern European" tropes — Georgia is its own place
